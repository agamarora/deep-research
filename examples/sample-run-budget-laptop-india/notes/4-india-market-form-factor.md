# Sub-question 4: India market — model availability + form factor

## Answer (TL;DR)
- The 14"-with-no-numpad-and-16:10 niche in India is dominated by exactly the cohort the user expected: **Lenovo ThinkBook 14 G7 / ThinkPad E14 G6 / ThinkPad T14 G5 / IdeaPad Slim 5 14, HP Pavilion Plus 14 (ew-series), ASUS Vivobook S14 OLED M5406 / Zenbook 14 OLED UX3405, Dell Inspiron 14 Plus 7440**. All ship with 14" 16:10 panels and no numpad.
- Within the **bare-config ₹50k–₹95k** budget, the realistic shortlist is ThinkPad E14 G6, IdeaPad Slim 5 14 (i5/13420H OLED), HP Pavilion Plus 14-ew1082TU, Dell Inspiron 14 Plus 7440 (Core 5 210H), and ThinkBook 14 G7 / 21SJA0K9IG — everything else (T14, Zenbook 14 OLED, Vivobook S14 256V) blows past ₹95k at base.
- **16" no-numpad is essentially nonexistent in Indian retail**: ThinkBook 16 G7 has a numpad (confirmed by Notebookcheck and Lenovo PSREF), and ThinkPad T16 also ships with a numpad in its standard layout. The only 16"-class "no-numpad" play is the ThinkBook 16+ / ThinkPad P16s, which are out of budget or not formally listed for India retail.
- Refurb/Renewed channels in India are usable but **mostly for Gen-1/2-old ThinkPads (~₹20–55k)**, not for current G7/Gen-6 stock; warranty is 6 months and quality is inconsistent (T3 source: edify.club).
- **India SKU caveat**: India ThinkBook/IdeaPad listings often default to **WUXGA OLED** at the i5-13420H tier (e.g., 14IRH10), whereas US listings emphasize WUXGA IPS Touch — the panel quality at the same price band is actually better in India for the OLED variants.

## Refurb / open-box channels

- **Amazon Renewed (India)**: Active for ThinkPad/ThinkBook stock, mainly **older generations (8th–12th gen, ₹20–60k)**. 6-month seller warranty. Quality varies by seller; "Premium / Excellent / Good" grading. Example: ThinkBook 15 12th-gen i7 at ₹57,798. Current G6/G7 stock is **rare** on Renewed. T2 source (edify.club, amazon.in renewed listings).
- **Flipkart Refurbished ("Flipkart 2GUD" / refurbished tag)**: Smaller catalog than Amazon Renewed, mostly mid-range Vivobook / IdeaPad. T3 source — coverage thin on premium business laptops.
- **Croma open-box**: Strictly in-store via individual outlets — no SKU-level online catalog. Best for short-return units of latest stock at 5–10% discount; not a reliable sourcing channel for a specific model.
- **Whynew.in / edify.club / others**: Independent refurb specialists with longer warranties (12 months) and grade transparency. Stock skews older (5th–10th gen ThinkPads at ₹15–30k). Useful for *cheap* T-series ThinkPads but not for current 2025/26 models.

**Verdict**: refurb is fine for older ThinkPads as a Linux-dev daily driver under ₹35k, but the 14" 16:10 ThinkBook G7 / E14 G6 / Pavilion Plus 14-ew tier is **not yet flowing into refurb** — buy new.

## India SKU vs global SKU notes

- **ThinkBook 14 G7 IML**: US Amazon listings emphasize WUXGA Touch IPS panels and Win 10/11 Pro; India SKUs (21SGA / 21SJA series) emphasize WUXGA IPS non-touch + Win 11 Home, slightly cheaper. 1Yr ADP often bundled in India.
- **ThinkPad E14 Gen 6**: Indian SKU 21M700AGIG (Core Ultra 5 125U) is closer to the European base than the US SKU; AMD Ryzen 7 7735U variant common in US is rarer in India retail (Lenovo.in lists len101t0095 as the AMD variant but stock fluctuates).
- **HP Pavilion Plus 14**: The "ew-series" in India includes 1920×1200 IPS, 2.2K (2240×1400) IPS, and 2.8K OLED panel options under similar SKU codes — verify the panel on the actual product page before purchase. 2.2K is the most common base-config panel on Indian shelves.
- **IdeaPad Slim 5 14IRH10**: India-only OLED-at-this-price configurations (Smartprix India listings consistently show OLED for the i5-13420H tier); US 14IRH equivalents often ship IPS at the same price.
