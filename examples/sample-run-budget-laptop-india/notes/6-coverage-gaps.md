# Sub-question 6 (correction round): coverage gaps

## 1. Webcam quality

- **ThinkPad E14 Gen 6**: Configurable as 720p HD or **FHD 1080p with privacy shutter** (and IR option on higher SKUs). XDA-Developers review of the 1080p sample called quality only "acceptable" — "the laptop can do video calls, but the image looks blurry and washed out," and Lenovo's Video Enhancer / Enhanced Clarity software was glitchy. Get the 1080p; the 720p is reportedly worse. ([xda-developers.com](https://www.xda-developers.com/lenovo-thinkpad-e14-gen-6-review/))
- **ThinkBook 14 G7 IML**: Three webcam SKUs per Lenovo PSREF — HD 720p, FHD 1080p, or **FHD 1080p + IR (Windows Hello)**, all with privacy shutter and fixed focus. NoypiGeeks/PCWorld describe the 1080p as a "high-quality user-facing webcam" — clearly the better config to spec. ([psref.lenovo.com](https://psref.lenovo.com/syspool/Sys/PDF/ThinkBook/ThinkBook_14_G7_IML/ThinkBook_14_G7_IML_Spec.pdf), [pcworld.com](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2833343/lenovo-thinkbook-14-review.html))
- **IdeaPad Slim 5 14IRH10**: **2 MP / FHD with IR + privacy shutter** on Indian SKUs (e.g. Reliance Digital listing). LaptopMedia: "lies just above the laptop standard… looks sharper than much of the competition, although it fails to deliver good color accuracy." ([laptopmedia.com](https://laptopmedia.com/series/lenovo-ideapad-slim-5-14irh10r/), [reliancedigital.in](https://www.reliancedigital.in/product/lenovo-ideapad-slim-5-14irh10-83hr000yin-standard-laptop-intel-core-i5-13420h16-gb1-tb-ssdintel-uhd-graphicswindows-11-homemsoffice-home-2024oled-3556-cm-14-inch-luna-grey-mb7sil-9214422))

## 2. Keyboard typing feel

- **ThinkPad E14 Gen 6**: Best of the three. XDA: "one of the best parts of any ThinkPad… just as responsive and pleasurable to type on… tactile, clicky, with rough texture for grip." Long-form typing endorsed. Backlit, spill-resistant, includes the TrackPoint nub. ([xda-developers.com](https://www.xda-developers.com/lenovo-thinkpad-e14-gen-6-review/))
- **ThinkBook 14 G7**: Redesigned this gen — wider keys with concave fingertip-shaped caps. Reviewers call it "comfortable… premium feel." Not quite ThinkPad-tier (no TrackPoint, slightly shallower travel) but a clear step up from IdeaPad. ([noypigeeks.com](https://www.noypigeeks.com/laptops/lenovo-thinkpad-e14-gen-6-review/), [pcworld.com](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2833343/lenovo-thinkbook-14-review.html))
- **IdeaPad Slim 5 14IRH10**: Backlit, "pleasant to type on," "decent key travel" per LaptopMedia/Beebom (Slim 5i family). Beebom noted it feels "slightly less tactile than other Lenovo models, though not mushy." ([beebom.com](https://beebom.com/lenovo-ideapad-slim-5i-review-a-powerful-workhorse/))

## 3. Build / chassis / hinge

- **ThinkPad E14 Gen 6**: Aluminum lid + aluminum chassis bottom, **MIL-STD-810H certified**, sturdy 180-degree hinge, spill-resistant deck. NoypiGeeks: "feels robust and durable just as a ThinkPad should." Best build of the three. ([noypigeeks.com](https://www.noypigeeks.com/laptops/lenovo-thinkpad-e14-gen-6-review/))
- **ThinkBook 14 G7**: Aluminum lid, plastic bottom — "solid build quality… but don't expect a metal lid" everywhere. PCWorld notes 180° hinge, spill-resistant keyboard, weight 1.38 kg. Mid-tier durability. ## 6. Battery hours under Linux

- **ThinkPad E14 Gen 6**: Notebookcheck/XDA Windows PCMark10 test = **5 h 36 min** (modest). Lenovo publishes a **Linux User Guide** for the E14 Gen 6 with Ubuntu 22.04 / RHEL drivers, indicating first-class Linux support. Community reports on adjacent T14/T14s suggest -10 to -20% vs Windows initially, recoverable to parity with TLP / `power-profiles-daemon`. Realistic expectation: **4.5–6 h** on a 57 Wh pack under mixed dev workload. ([notebookcheck.net](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Highly-upgradeable-and-affordable-Lenovo-ThinkPad-E14-Gen-6-AMD-laptop-review.953041.0.html), [Lenovo Linux UG PDF](https://download.lenovo.com/pccbbs/mobiles_pdf/e14_g6_e16_g2_linux_ug.pdf))
- **ThinkBook 14 G7 IML**: Notebookcheck-cited Windows test ≈ **10 hours of video playback** on the 60 Wh battery — best of the three on paper. No published Linux benchmark; expect **6–8 h** mixed dev work after Linux discount. ([notebookcheck.net external reviews](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Lenovo-ThinkBook-14-G7-IML-Ultra-5-125U.909638.0.html))
- **IdeaPad Slim 5 14IRH10 (OLED)**: OLED + 13420H Raptor Lake-H is power-hungry. Notebookcheck OLED variant pegs runtime around **7–8 h** browsing on Windows; users on the OLED report **3–4 h** during video playback at full brightness. Linux figures unpublished. ([notebookcheck.net](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Lenovo-IdeaPad-Slim-5-14-laptop-review-A-successful-allrounder-with-an-OLED-display.813094.0.html))

## 7. Thermal noise

- **ThinkPad E14 Gen 6**: Notebookcheck on prior E14 generations: fan stays under ~30 dB(A) under load — quiet. Gen 6 reviews don't flag noise as an issue; sustained-load throttling occurs but acoustics aren't called out as problematic. ([notebookcheck.net E14 historical](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Lenovo-ThinkPad-E14-Laptop-Review-Thin-design-beats-upgradability.455279.0.html))
- **ThinkBook 14 G7 IML**: LaptopMedia: "the fan isn't noisy and the keyboard becomes just warm when the processor is doing the heavy lifting." Quietest of the three under load. ([laptopmedia.com](https://laptopmedia.com/review/lenovo-thinkbook-14-gen-7-intel-review-performs-like-a-16-inch-business-device/))
- **IdeaPad Slim 5 14IRH10**: H-class CPU (i5-13420H / i7-13620H) in a 16.9 mm chassis — fans **do** ramp under sustained compile loads. LaptopMedia (sister model 14 AMD) flags "long battery life, quiet performance," but the Intel-H IRH10 variant in this thin chassis is noisier under sustained load than the AMD U-series equivalents.

## 8. India warranty + service

- **Lenovo**: Default = **1-yr carry-in warranty** (sometimes "1-yr ADP" on consumer SKUs like the OLED IdeaPad). Onsite is an upgrade (~₹2k–₹5k for 1-yr to 3-yr Premier upgrade). Official service-center map covers Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi, Pune, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, Ahmedabad, Noida, Gurugram, Visakhapatnam + 25 more cities — densest network of the four OEMs. ([lenovo.com/in service](https://www.lenovo.com/in/en/services/), [Lenovo IN warranty upgrades](https://www.lenovo.com/in/en/warranty-services/))
- **Dell**: ProSupport offers **Next-Business-Day Onsite** as standard on most business SKUs in tier-1 cities; 4-hr/8-hr SLAs available on enterprise lines. Strong onsite reputation but bundled cost is higher. ([dell.com/en-in](https://www.dell.com/support/contractservices/en-in/))
- **HP**: Onsite available via HP Care Pack add-on; comparable city coverage. ASUS has the **thinnest** service network of the four — consumer feedback on ASUS turn-around in tier-2/3 is consistently weakest.
- **Net**: For a Linux dev who may need to RMA a faulty motherboard, **Lenovo ThinkPad with the 3-yr Premier Onsite upgrade** in Bangalore/Mumbai/Delhi is the lowest-friction option.
